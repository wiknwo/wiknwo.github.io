Things You Hear Too Late In Life

This is a short and non-exhaustive list on some things you hear too late in life.


  • First impressions do matter because you MIGHT only meet someone once or MIGHT only have one chance and you MIGHT not know.
  • Exams should be embraced and your perspective on them should change. View it as a chance to improve your time-management skills.
  • "There is no time like the present, a thousand unforeseen circumstances may interrupt you at a future time" - John Trusier i.e. Or as my Mom would say "Events have overtaken" (Do it now rather than later)
  • If you really wanted to do something then you would have done it already.
  • Work with time; the world operates on faith and trust.
  • Time is of the essence.
  • We don't have time.
  • Humans break.
  • When it comes to doing something for yourself, investing in yourself; you reap what you sew i.e. you put in what you get out.
  • Make necessary changes immediately.
  • The odds are against you, things aren't in your favour, the system wasn't designed for you to get there in the first place, life is unfair, it's not straightforward and there is no obligation for anything to be given to you. It must be obtained or striven for.
  • Be bold and genuine.

Golden Time

Postponing and Procrastination
Hand in hand, sisters of sloth
Much like "clothes" without "cloth"
An unfinished word -
An unfinished word
With intention of completion
Few moments later - stifling stagnation
A constant chronicle, going no where
With no occurences to document.
Or at least some without subsequent

A deadly sin, just one of the seven
It's paradoxical twin
Patience. The virtue; associated with Heaven
Stark and stellar opposites
Similar on the surface,
Often the two are confused

However, they each have their respective identity
Almost indistinguishable in character
Nevertheless, there is a difference -
Even though it be an inkling.

In fact, the slothful are the most impatient
For they have nothing to wait for
They are too impatient to even begin.
For a sloth, postpones the beginning
In an attempt to reach the end.
For the best of us;
Tis only a matter of time.
A time we must await.
Anticipate.
Brace.

"Good things come to those who wait but only the things left by those who hustle"
- Abe Lincoln

StudentCorner

Some tips, tricks, reflections and advice for students.

My Undergraduate Reflections

  • Be aware of your seating choice.
  • Have an awareness of those listening to you and watching when you are asking questions in class.
  • Keep comments/opinions about lecture attendance, exams, assignments, emails and etc. to yourself and don't offer the freely unless explicitly asked.
  • In group work assignments, ensure your part is done.
  • For big projects that may take up too much time and are not so straight-forward, always try to have something to show for your efforts as much as possible by bagging the 'low hanging fruit' and compiling results for those then tackle the more challenging portions of the project afterwards. Do this in case something unexpected happens so you still have marks for your project, especially the Final Year Project (FYP).
  • Make your learning your own from start to finish. Do not allow a third party to dictate your learning preferences. Your degree is what you make of it!
  • Look into the type of degree that will be awarded and meaning of the grade levels.
  • The Junior year is a bit more intense as modules increase in workload and complexity, adequately prepare for this by putting appropriate measures in place and ensuring you take care of your mental health to keep yourself in tact.
  • The Final Year is what makes a the degree an honours degree, it is the most intense year where significant effort will have to be expended and new challenges and changes will be introduced. Pace yourself, as you will be spread thin whilst attending to coursework and simultaneously job hunting. Watch your health, prioritise your well-being, stay organized, meet the tight deadlines and hand in work on time and done well with the 'little things' that add bells and whistles to your assignment that make it standout and will earn you those much needed few extra marks to get the first i.e. presentation, summarization, going above and beyond (will probably write about this soon). This is the time to cement your years of study by plowing, driving steadily and assuredly to the end not time to fluff and take your foot off the pedal and drop the ball. Make sure those closest to you are well aware of the demands and commitments being made by you in this final year. Ad astra.
  • Regulate yourself, have a focused and measured intensity about yourself. Balance, breaks, efficiency, productivity and moderation. Don't exert yourself too much if you don't have to. Be incisive instead and learn how you learn.
  • Less is more.
  • Mind who you show yourself to.
  • Curiosity and interest are KING.
  • Benefit of the doubt: State of accepting something/someone as honest or deserving of trust even though there are doubts they might be lying.
  • Stress is real and its not just mental, it is a bodily response.
  • First (Freshman) Year: Build foundation in major
  • Second (Sophomore) Year: Build foundation in major
  • Third (Junior) Year: Increase intensity of modules and make transition of modules towards industry.
  • Fourth (Senior) Year: Most employable, intense and challenging year. Some modules are at the Masters level (Stage 4) with less demonstrator support, increased intensity from Third Year and all modules are at the level of industry being topical for that specific year.
  • You are on your own; take your learning and exam preparations into your own hands.
  • Make friends with other serious students.
  • Your degree is what you make of it.
  • Chart your own course, blaze your own trail, follow your own path, lead your own life and take the bull by the horns.
  • Make up your own mind, see for yourself, think for yourself, make your own judgements, make your own assessments.

My Undergraduate Mistakes

  • Not getting the grades I deserved/not doing myself justice by just not collecting marks on purpose. Unnecessary self-inflicted grade loss. Collect the marks wherever you can and get the grade you deserve. You are more than capable. It only goes to your advantage. So get the grades that are reflective of your ablities, if not for you then at least for your parents, employers, graduate school etc.
  • Submitting assignments late
  • Not getting the grades I could because I wasn't interested in the material. You don't have to like a module to get the grades, find another purpose behind why you are taking the module i.e. to make sure you have good grades for graduate school. Gain perspective.
  • Not getting the grades I could because I didn't follow the lecturer's assiged method. Just follow the lecturer's method and do your own method too. Originality is always a plus.
